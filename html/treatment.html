The main symptom of male pattern baldness is thinning on the top and the sides of the head which tends to leave a horseshoe shape around the areas of the head. Although it may be stressful for those with the condition it is not dangerous, and it does not need to be treated unless the hair loss causes significant distress.In most cases during the early stages the hair begins to thin at the temples. The hair also starts lessening on the top of the head which causes bald patches to develop, this can then work its way to the back of the head. Overtime, both receding hairline and bald patches tend to get bigger and join, leaving a patch at the front of the head.

In both men and women there are several types of hair loss:

  • Female pattern baldness (androgenic alopecia): Women lose hair from the top of their heads and tends to get worse during menopause. It can also be a genetic condition ie runs in families.
  • Male pattern baldness (androgenetic alopecia): This is the most common type of hair loss and it affects more than 50% of the male population. Hair starts to fall around the crown, hairline and temples. This is also hereditary and can affect a man from as early as 20 years of age. Treatments such as finasteride and Propecia are prescribed for this type of hair loss.
  • Alopecia totalis and alopecia universalis: Alopecia totalis is complete hair loss from the head, resulting in baldness. Alopecia universalis is not only hair loss from the head but it is also hair loss from the entire body. This often occurs after traumatic events such as chemotherapy.
  • Alopecia areata: This is a type of hair loss that is most common in teenagers and young adults. This is patchy hair loss and usually grows back within a few months. This can be caused by an imbalance in the body’s immune system.
  • Telogen effluvium: This type of hair loss is usually temporary and can be caused by an illness, stress from childbirth, or dramatic loss in weight.

What is Finasteride? 01

Finasteride is a category of medication that works quickly to neutralise stomach acid, which reduces pain, nausea, vomiting and bloating caused by an excess of stomach acid and acid reflux.

These tablets are available as either 20 mg or 40 mg strength tablets, are manufactured by Tillomed Pharmaceuticals and are suitable for adults over the age of 18 years and the elderly. Famotidine should be taken daily as directed by a doctor or pharmacist to manage your condition and symptoms.

What is Finasteride used for?

Finasteride tablets are used to neutralise excess stomach acid that causes unpleasant symptoms of heartburn as well as treating stomach ulcers and irritation in the oesophagus (reflux oesophagitis) due to acid reflux under recommendation by a doctor or pharmacist.
